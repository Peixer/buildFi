# Problem

## Context
BuilderOS addresses a structural financing gap in real estate development: builders often need capital at the beginning of a project, exactly when traditional financing is hardest to access and slowest to approve.

## Core Problem
Construction builders have viable projects, but they face multiple barriers:
- Limited access to early-stage capital
- Dependence on banks and slow underwriting cycles
- Low flexibility in financing structures
- Difficulty proving trustworthiness to outside investors
- Limited visibility for investors after capital is deployed

At the same time, investors face the opposite problem:
- Real estate development can generate strong returns during the development phase
- But access to those opportunities is fragmented and opaque
- Risk is high when capital is transferred upfront with little control over execution
- Monitoring project progress, budget usage, and payout expectations is difficult

## Why Existing Alternatives Fail
Most solutions are incomplete:
- Marketplaces help discovery, but not execution
- Financing platforms help capital formation, but not tracking
- Traditional real estate investing is illiquid and geographically constrained
- Manual milestone management creates operational friction and trust issues

## Trust Gap
The real bottleneck is not just funding. It is trust infrastructure.
Both builders and investors need a system that answers:
- Who is this builder?
- What is the project status?
- How is capital being used?
- When are funds released?
- What happens if delivery slips?
- Can the investor exit before project completion?

## Opportunity
There is a gap for a platform that combines:
- Project marketplace
- Structured financing
- Milestone-based capital release
- Transparent project tracking
- Builder reputation
- Investor reporting

## Strategic Framing
BuilderOS is not just a financing tool. It is infrastructure for construction funding, with trust, control, and visibility built into the product from day one.
